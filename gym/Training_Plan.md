## Progression

- **Add reps first** — when you can do 12 clean reps, bump the weight slightly
- **Slow the eccentric** — 2-3 second lowering phase builds more muscle
- **Chase the pump and squeeze** — if you don't feel the target muscle working, the weight is too heavy

## When to Push Weight Over Mind-Muscle Connection

- **Compound leg movements** (leg press, split squats, hip thrusts) — legs respond well to progressive load
- **Rows and pulldowns** — once you've established the connection, load up a bit more
- **When stuck at the same weight for 3-4 weeks** — push the load up even if reps drop to 8, then build back to 12
- **Rule of thumb:** if you can't do at least 8 controlled reps with full ROM and feel the target muscle, the weight is too heavy

## Cycling Guidelines

- **80/20 rule:** ~80% easy (Zone 2), ~20% hard efforts
- **Separate hard from hard** — don't do a brutal hill ride before leg day
- **Zone 2** builds aerobic base without eating into gym recovery
- **Scale up over time:** start with 2-3 rides/week, build toward 3-4 with one long weekend ride
- **If recovery suffers:** cut a gym day before cutting a ride

## Nutrition

- Eat enough protein (~0.7-1g per lb bodyweight)
- Cycling burns a lot of calories — don't accidentally run a big deficit
- No need to track obsessively, just don't undereat
