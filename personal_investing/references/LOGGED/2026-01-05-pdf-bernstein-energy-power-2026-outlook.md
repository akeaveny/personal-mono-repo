# Bernstein Energy & Power: Look No Further for Defensive Growth — 2026 Outlook

## Summary

Bernstein's 25-page European utilities outlook for 2026 positions the sector as the best place for defensive growth — trading at a ~9% P/E discount to the European market with ~7% 3-year EPS CAGR and a ~120bps dividend yield uplift (4.2% vs 3.0% market). Utilities were the 2nd best performing European sector in 2025 (+34%). Top picks are electric networks (SSE, National Grid, Redeia, Terna) and renewables (EDP, EDPR, RWE, Engie). The report warns heavily about a looming LNG supply glut (130 MTPA, or 30% of global demand, coming online 2025-2027) and argues data center demand growth won't offset European power demand lost to de-industrialization since 2022.

## Key Claims

1. **LNG supply glut is massive and imminent.** ~130 MTPA of additional liquefaction capacity coming online 2025-2027, equivalent to adding 30% of global LNG demand. If combined with end of Russia-Ukraine war and re-commencement of Russian gas flows, LNG prices could decline to marginal cost of supply (~$5-9/MMBTU). US mid-cycle gas price target: ~$5/MMBTU.

2. **Data center demand won't save European power markets.** Even under the most optimistic forecasts, data center demand growth falls short of replacing the ~149 TWh of demand lost to European de-industrialization since 2019. Europe needs more than data centers to return power markets to growth.

3. **Nuclear life extensions are a growing theme.** Belgium (Engie — Tihange 3/Doel 4 to 2035, possibly further), Spain, and UK considering extensions. Nordics already assumed done deals. Utilities repurposing decommissioned thermal sites for data centers (Engie identified 1,000+ grid-connected sites).

4. **Electric networks are the best risk-adjusted play.** High earnings visibility, compelling growth outlook for T&D investment to 2030, and minimal exposure to commodity price downside. Grid investments for AI/data centers represent upside not yet in forecasts.

5. **US onshore renewables are a bright spot.** OBBBA signing and IRS safe-harbor guidance provide clarity — projects safe-harbored before mid-2026 have until mid-2030 for service. EDPR has 5GW+ safe-harbored, RWE 8-10GW, Orsted 6-7GW.

6. **EU carbon prices at highest since Oct 2023.** EU ETS above €87/tonne with funds at record net long positions (110M tonnes). 2026 sees 8.6% emission cap reduction, maritime shipping 100% included, declining auction supply.

## What I'm Taking Away

- The LNG supply glut is now being flagged by multiple sources (Piper Sandler + Bernstein). 130 MTPA is staggering — this is a clear risk factor for Cheniere (LNG) and should lower conviction on that name. Monitor closely.
- European data center demand growth is overhyped relative to the de-industrialization demand hole. Be cautious about AI-driven power demand narratives driving investment decisions.
- Nuclear life extensions are a distinct sub-theme from new nuclear build/SMRs — both reinforce CCJ/uranium thesis but on different timelines.
- The report is primarily relevant for European utility investors; for North American satellite portfolio, the main takeaways are LNG supply glut risk and confirmation that defensive/income strategies are in favor globally.
