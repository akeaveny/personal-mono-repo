## Market Outlook: Defensive — Energy Conviction Elevated

The overall posture remains **defensive**, but with a key upgrade: the energy supply disruption trigger has **fired**. Gulf War III closed the Strait of Hormuz, damaged Qatar LNG infrastructure, and pushed Brent to $100+. The thesis called for adding to energy on exactly this signal — and XLE is +30% YTD validating the overweight.

Meanwhile, SPY is only -4.3% YTD (no broad correction), the Fed is on hold, and US-China tariffs have escalated to 145%. The macro environment is more hostile than in March, reinforcing the defensive posture everywhere except energy and hard assets.

**Core conviction:** Energy and hard assets (gold, uranium, materials) are the trades. Broad equities are expensive and fragile. Semis are finally offering selective entry points after individual names corrected 30-60%. Stay disciplined on position sizing.

## Macro Environment

### Geopolitical Risk — Extreme (Upgraded from Elevated)
- **Gulf War III (Feb-March 2026):** Strait of Hormuz closed since early March; Qatar LNG output damaged (~12.8 MT lost for 3-5 years); 20% of global oil disrupted with 0% spare capacity
- **US-Iran ceasefire (April 8):** Two-week truce only. Brent crashed 13% from $110 to $96 on the news, then recovered to $102. Fragile — any collapse re-escalates immediately
- **US-China tariffs at 145%** on Chinese imports (up from 104% in early April). Supreme Court struck down IEEPA tariffs in Feb, but Section 301 investigations launched in March. Trade has contracted ~$170B
- Reshoring is no longer theoretical — it's being forced by tariff policy. Inflationary in the short term, structurally bullish for US industrials and materials
- Energy security has moved from "concern" to "crisis" — this is the single biggest macro driver right now

### Recession Risk — Elevated but Not Confirmed
- Consumer fatigue deepening under energy price shock (gasoline, heating, food transport costs)
- Fed stuck at 3.5-3.75% — inflation from energy/tariffs prevents easing even as growth slows (stagflation risk)
- Only 1 rate cut expected in 2026. Next FOMC meeting April 28-29
- Credit conditions tightening. S&P 500 still at 22x fwd PE (vs 17.1x 30-year avg) — not pricing in recession
- SPY -4.3% YTD is not a correction. If recession materializes, the downside is significant from here

### Hard Assets — Breakout
- Gold at **$4,743/oz** — up 25%+ since early 2025. Central bank buying, inflation hedge, geopolitical safe haven
- Silver at **$77/oz** — outperforming gold on industrial + monetary demand
- Uranium spot collapsed to **$63/lb** from $101 peak (Jan), but BofA targets $130 by Q4 2026. Uncovered utility requirements at record levels
- Precious metals and commodities are the clearest beneficiaries of the current macro regime

## Principles

1. **Don't force trades.** If nothing screams opportunity, holding cash in the satellite bucket is a valid position.
2. **Respect the drift threshold.** Let the bot flag when rebalancing is needed rather than reacting to daily noise.
3. **Thesis reviews are quarterly.** Revisit this document every quarter and update the sector views.
4. **Core never changes on a whim.** The 80% ETF base is the foundation — only adjust targets after a full thesis review.
5. **Size positions to sleep at night.** No single satellite position should cause stress if it drops 30%.
6. **When a trigger fires, act on it.** The whole point of pre-defining triggers is to remove emotion from the decision. Energy disruption fired — deploy.
7. **Scale in, don't go all-in.** Especially for semis and speculative materials names. Build positions over weeks, not in one trade.
