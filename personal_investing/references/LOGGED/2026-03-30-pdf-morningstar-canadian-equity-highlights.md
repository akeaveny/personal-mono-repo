# Morningstar Canadian Equity Highlights — March 2, 2026

## Summary

44-page earnings roundup covering Canadian coverage as of Feb 27, 2026. The key finding across sectors: Materials (gold/copper miners) are trading at 2-4x Morningstar fair value on spot prices they view as unsustainable, Canadian banks beat on trading income but Morningstar warns against extrapolating it, and the IT/Communication sectors — the most underweight in XIC — hold the most 5-star undervalued names.

## Key Claims

1. **Gold miners severely overvalued.** Spot gold at ~$5,000-5,200/oz vs. Morningstar's $2,050 long-run midcycle estimate. Barrick trades at 1.65x FV, Agnico at 2.74x, Kinross at 3.88x. These valuations assume spot gold stays permanently elevated.
2. **Canadian banks beat Q1 on trading income, but Morningstar flags it as unsustainable.** All Big 6 are 2-star (overvalued). Strong capital markets results drove EPS beats, but normalized ROEs remain below medium-term targets. Don't extrapolate.
3. **IT and Communication are the most undervalued sectors on the TSX.** Constellation Software (CSU) at 0.66x FV, Thomson Reuters (TRI) at 0.56x FV (down 50%+ on AI fears — Morningstar says reaction is overdone), Shopify at 0.76x FV, Descartes at 0.67x FV, CGI at 0.70x FV. BCE at 0.80x FV, Telus at 0.62x FV, Rogers at 0.78x FV (all 5-star).
4. **Thomson Reuters: wide-moat 5-star at 0.56x FV.** Shares crushed 50%+ from July 2025 peak on AI fears (Claude legal plug-in debut Feb 3 caused 16% drop in one day). Morningstar maintains fair value — the plug-in doesn't touch legal research, which is TRI's core moat. 2026 organic revenue guidance: 7.5-8%, EBITDA margin expanding 100bps.
5. **Copper (Teck) also overvalued.** Copper at $5.90/lb vs. $3.80 midcycle. Teck merging with Anglo American. The energy transition demand thesis is priced in.
6. **Energy pipelines (Enbridge, Pembina) fairly valued.** ENB at 1.10x FV, PPL at 1.08x FV — 3-star territory. E&P names (CNQ at 1.39x, TOU at 1.23x) are overvalued.
7. **Consumer Discretionary is a pocket of value.** RBI (QSR) at 0.89x FV (4-star), Gildan (GIL) at 0.73x FV (4-star), Spin Master (TOY) at 0.53x FV (5-star).
8. **Brookfield Asset Management (BAM) undervalued at 0.82x FV** (4-star). Solid quarter, $603B fee-earning AUM, fundraising above run rate.

## Thesis Impact

- **Impact:** SUPPORTS
- **Tickers affected:** All TSX-listed positions and watchlist names
- **Claims touched:**
  - *STRONGLY SUPPORTS* — Canadian sector concentration risk thesis. Financials + Materials = ~53% of XIC, both broadly overvalued (banks 2-star, gold miners 1-star). This is the core argument for adding international diversification (XSP/XEF).
  - *SUPPORTS* — IT and Communication as satellite candidates. Morningstar's own data confirms CSU, TRI, Shopify, BCE, Telus, Rogers are the cheapest names on the TSX with the smallest XIC weights.
  - *NEW CANDIDATE* — Thomson Reuters (TRI). Wide-moat 5-star at 0.56x FV. Not currently on STOCKS.md or THESIS.md. The AI disruption narrative is overblown per Morningstar — the Claude legal plug-in targets in-house legal teams, not legal research (TRI's core moat). Down 50%+ = potential entry point.
  - *NEUTRAL* — Energy pipelines (Enbridge, Pembina, TC Energy) are fairly to slightly overvalued. Not a compelling buy but not a sell either.
  - *SUPPORTS* — Avoid doubling down on gold miners. All 1-star with Price/FV of 1.6-3.9x.

## What I'm Taking Away

- Thomson Reuters (TRI) deserves a proper look. Wide moat, 5-star, 0.56x FV, down 50% on AI fears that Morningstar doesn't buy. Could add to STOCKS.md as a `#tech` `#candidate`.
- CSU.TO — already on watchlist. Morningstar confirms 5-star at 0.66x FV. The thesis is intact.
- The banks' strong Q1 was largely trading income (CIBC +42% YoY, TD wholesale +65%). Morningstar explicitly says don't extrapolate. XIC's 30% financial weighting is a risk if trading income normalizes.
- Gold miners: the market is pricing in $5,000+ gold permanently. Morningstar says midcycle is $2,050. The spread is extreme — avoid.
