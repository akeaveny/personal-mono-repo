# European Overview — Not Just Sunshine and Rainbows

## Summary

Barclays' January 2026 European credit outlook expects spreads to widen modestly, with Europe outperforming the US in spread terms. The macro backdrop remains benign but deteriorating US technicals (principally supply-led) and tight starting valuations will weigh on spreads. High all-in yields still support positive total returns. The report covers five areas of client disagreement: US supply technicals, US vs Europe preference, bank sector views, best carry sources, and private credit risks.

## Key Claims

1. **European credit spreads to widen modestly in 2026.** Tight starting valuations and increased US supply will push spreads wider globally, but Europe should outperform the US. Positive total returns still expected thanks to high all-in yields.

2. **3-month FX hedging costs from USD to EUR projected to decline meaningfully.** If the priced rate path (Fed/ECB) prevails, this benefits US credit for yield-focused European investors and could shift flows.

3. **2028 leveraged loan maturity wall is formidable.** The highest as a percentage of the market since 2012, making leveraged loans vulnerable to any persistent deterioration in sentiment.
