# Canadian Energy Infrastructure Overview Tables — January 2026

## Summary

Peters & Co.'s 7-page infrastructure overview provides comprehensive comp tables for 15 Canadian energy infrastructure names across three categories: Pipelines/Midstream (ENB, TRP, PPL, ALA, KEY, SOBO, GEI, RGSI), Renewables/Power (BEP.U, CPX, TA, NPI, BLX), and Other Infrastructure (TPZ, SES). Coverage median shows 4.8% dividend yield, 11.3x EV/DAFCF, and 6.5% implied NAV yield. Data-heavy reference with capitalization, yield & growth vs valuation, and financial metrics tables.

## Key Claims

1. **Canadian midstream/pipelines are income workhorses.** Median 5.4% dividend yield with 11.9x EV/DAFCF. Top picks (Outperform): PPL (5.4% yield, $58 target), KEY (4.9%, $52), SOBO (7.4%, $44), GEI (6.8%, $28), RGSI (4.2%, $31). ENB and TRP both Sector Perform.

2. **Renewables/Power trade at lower multiples but more growth.** Median 4.0% yield, 9.4x EV/DAFCF. Capital Power (CPX) and TransAlta (TA) both Outperform with significant upside (CPX target $73 vs $59, TA target $27 vs $18). BEP.U is Brookfield-controlled (47%) with 14.3x EV/DAFCF.

3. **Yield + growth math favors SOBO, GEI, and TA.** SOBO offers 13.2% total return (7.4% yield + 2.8% growth + 3.0% buyback). GEI at 14.6% (6.8% + 5.8% + 1.3%). TA at 36.9% (1.5% + 23.7% growth + 9.2% buyback) — highest total return potential but lowest yield.

4. **Sector is well-capitalized.** Total coverage enterprise value C$598.5B. Pipelines dominate at C$502.7B EV. Median net debt/EBITDA of 4.8x across pipelines — leverage is manageable.
