# Spectrum of Energy — Year-End 2025 Review

## Summary

Piper Sandler's 43-page year-end "Spectrum of Energy" report reviews 2025 across four pillars: OPEC's political decision to pump for Trump (deflating oil prices), the emergence of geopolitical multipolarity (Donroe Doctrine, Ukraine, Middle East), the Trump administration's gutting of green energy subsidies ($560B in tax credits, ~2,000 projects/266GW cancelled), and green shoots in nuclear, LNG, batteries, and AI power demand. The report also provides tactical sub-sector preferences and natural gas price forecasts.

## Key Claims

1. **OPEC's supply surge was political, not economic.** Global oil supply grew +3.5 mb/d y/y by Q4 2025, dwarfing +1.1 mb/d demand growth. OPEC added 1.5 mb/d it didn't need to — driven by geopolitical alignment with Trump (protection, AI chips, civilian nukes, arms). Oil, gasoline, LNG, and coal prices all deflated.

2. **Nuclear renaissance is real and accelerating.** Japan's Kashiwazaki-Kariwa (8.2 GW, world's largest nuclear plant) cleared its final hurdle and will restart partially in 2026. SMR momentum continues with US policy backing (string of Executive Orders). Data center power demand is driving the nuclear narrative.

3. **Green energy is being destroyed in the US.** Trump's One Big Beautiful Bill eviscerated ~$560B in green energy tax credits. ~2,000 projects (266 GW) cancelled — utility-scale solar (86 GW), battery storage (79 GW), wind (54 GW). Offshore wind is functionally dead. Ford took a $19.5B charge and abandoned large truck/SUV electrification. US BEV share of new car sales plummeted post-subsidy removal.

4. **US LNG dominance is established but a supply glut is emerging.** US became the world's largest LNG exporter in 2025. However, Energy Transfer cancelled its 16.5 mtpa Lake Charles terminal, citing better risk/return from pipeline infrastructure. LNG spot prices falling going into winter (normally tight).

5. **Tactical sub-sector preferences favor refining and integrated oils.** Top ranks: US refining (#1 — tight distillates, low feedstock costs), integrated oils (#2 — fortified balance sheets, dividends), international/offshore oil services (#3 — capex expectations improving). Bottom: Lower 48 oil services (#7), consumer levered renewables (#12).

6. **Natural gas price targets cut by ~$0.75/MMBtu.** New 2026 forecast range $2.75-$3.75, long-term $3.50 (Haynesville breakeven). Summer 2026 needs to price below Summer 2025 to avoid overfill. Producer discipline remains "an oxymoron."

## What I'm Taking Away

- OPEC's willingness to pump for political alignment is a real risk to energy price bulls — the geopolitical floor is softer than assumed when OPEC is incentivized to add supply. This doesn't invalidate the energy overweight but tempers upside expectations for crude-levered names (OXY, DVN).
- Refining (VLO) and integrated oils (CVX) are the better defensive energy plays — tight distillate fundamentals and diversified earnings provide downside protection even in a lower crude price environment.
- Nuclear thesis is getting stronger with each report — Japan restart is a major signal. CCJ remains the top uranium play.
- LNG supply glut is a real risk for Cheniere (LNG) — Energy Transfer cancelling Lake Charles is a canary in the coal mine. Monitor spot LNG prices closely.
- Natural gas at $2.75-$3.75 means gassy E&Ps are less attractive — consistent with thesis to favor oily names.
