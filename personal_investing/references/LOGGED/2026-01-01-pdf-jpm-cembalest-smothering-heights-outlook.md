# Smothering Heights: Is the Largest Moat in Market History Indestructible?

## Summary

Michael Cembalest's 53-page 2026 annual outlook from J.P. Morgan examines the unprecedented concentration of US equity markets around AI. 42 AI-related companies have generated 65-75% of S&P 500 returns, earnings, and capital spending since ChatGPT's launch. Eight hyperscalers and semiscalers (NVDA, TSMC, ASML, MSFT, GOOG, AMZN, META, AMD) have grown from $3T to $18T market cap in seven years, representing ~20% of developed world equity markets. The report walks through four "What Could Go Wrong" risks: a Metaverse moment for hyperscaler profits, US power generation constraints, China scaling the moat with its own tech, and China-Taiwan risk. Despite $1.3T in capex/R&D since 2022, Cembalest notes valuations are roughly half of dotcom peak levels and tech PEG ratios are 1-3x vs 4-8x then. Expects 10-15% correction in 2026 followed by markets ending higher.

## Key Claims

1. **AI concentration has "smothered" the rest of the US equity market.** Without the 42 AI stocks, the S&P 500 would have underperformed Europe, Japan, and China. Tech capital spending contributed 40-45% of US GDP growth in Q2-Q3 2025, up from <5% in early 2023. The capex surge now exceeds the dotcom era as a share of GDP.

2. **"Metaverse moment" is the most immediate risk.** $1.3T in hyperscaler capex/R&D since Q4 2022. If this investment doesn't generate commensurate profits, moat companies could face a 50% decline like the Mag 7 in 2022. Meta's capex+R&D at 70% of revenue (vs 10% median S&P 500 company). Oracle and Meta starting to finance data centers via debt/off-balance-sheet SPVs rather than free cash flow.

3. **US power generation is a real constraint.** Listed as WCGW #2. Data center power demand is surging (see prior JPM "Fighting Words" entry on gas turbines sold out through 2030). This is a bottleneck that could slow AI buildout and benefits energy/power infrastructure.

4. **China scaling the moat is "when, not if."** Chinese AI models closing the gap rapidly (Artificial Analysis shows China models approaching US performance). DeepSeek and others progressing. ASML and TSMC are the critical chokepoints.

5. **Valuations are high but less stretched than they appear.** Tech P/E ~half of dotcom peak. Higher profitability (tech FCF margins 25-30% vs 5-10% for S&P 500 ex-tech) justifies higher multiples — the P/E-to-ROE relationship is internally consistent. PEG ratios for global tech are only modestly above the broad market. 2025 S&P 500 gain was 3% multiple expansion + 15% earnings growth — mostly earned.

6. **2026 forecast: 10-15% correction then markets end higher.** Similar to 2025 pattern — profit-taking and growth scare mid-year, then recovery. Medium-term moat risks are the ones to watch, not short-term valuations.

- **Impact:** SUPPORTS

- **Claims touched:**
  - *SUPPORTS* — "AI infrastructure buildout is real, but valuations have run far ahead of monetization" — $1.3T in capex without commensurate profit follow-through is exactly the thesis concern. Metaverse moment risk is the key downside.
  - *SUPPORTS* — "Not selling existing positions, but not adding until the picture clarifies" — Cembalest sees medium-term risks but not immediate collapse. The "wait for 15%+ pullback" entry trigger remains correct.
  - *SUPPORTS* — Defensive market posture — the extreme AI concentration means the S&P 500 is effectively a leveraged AI bet. Diversification into energy, value, and international reduces this concentration risk.
  - *SUPPORTS* — "Uranium deserves attention as nuclear gains policy support globally" — US power generation constraints (WCGW #2) will drive demand for nuclear, gas, and grid infrastructure.

## What I'm Taking Away

- The thesis approach of "watch only" on AI/semis is validated. The moat is real but fragile — $1.3T capex needs to convert to profits or it's Metaverse 2.0. Don't chase.
- The expected 10-15% correction is exactly the "trigger to upgrade" — if it materializes, that's the window to add NVDA, TSM, AVGO, or MRVL.
- Power constraints as WCGW #2 directly supports the energy overweight thesis — AI buildout needs energy, and energy infrastructure is the bottleneck.
- S&P 500 = concentrated AI bet. The core ETF allocation (VTI + VXUS + BND + VNQ) provides some diversification but VTI is itself 34% IT. Consider this when evaluating overall portfolio AI exposure.
