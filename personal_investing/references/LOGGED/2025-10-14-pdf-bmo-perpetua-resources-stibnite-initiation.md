# Perpetua Resources (PPTA-TSX) — Initiating Coverage at Outperform: Making Money From Antimony

## Summary

BMO Capital Markets initiates coverage on Perpetua Resources (PPTA-TSX) with Outperform rating and C$38.00 target. The Stibnite Project in Idaho is the largest standalone gold resource in the USA — 4.8 Moz Au reserves at 1.43 g/t plus 149 Mlbs antimony (critical mineral for DoD). NPV of $2.9B at 5% discount rate. First production expected 2029 with 288K oz/yr average at AISC $841/oz over 15 years. Key differentiator: mostly non-dilutive financing via $2B EXIM Bank loan and US government backing ("America First" critical minerals policy). The project is fully permitted and also serves as an environmental restoration of a 100+ year mining legacy.

## Key Claims

1. **Stibnite is a rare fully-permitted, government-backed gold developer.** Largest standalone gold resource in the US. EXIM Bank $2B debt facility (spring 2026 decision) dramatically reduces equity dilution. Trading at 1.2x P/NAV vs 0.7x developer average — premium justified by permit certainty and financing visibility.

2. **Antimony is the strategic kicker.** 78 Mlbs LOM production. 5-10% of bulk concentrate meets DoD needs for antimony trisulfide. Three salable downstream products (trisulfide, trioxide, metal). US has near-zero domestic antimony production — China dominates supply. Critical mineral designation provides policy tailwinds.

3. **Economics are strong even at conservative assumptions.** Cash costs $643/oz (by-product), AISC $841/oz. Initial capex US$2.5B. At BMO's gold price assumptions ($4,401/oz for 2026), the project generates substantial free cash flow. Higher grades (1.4 g/t average) in early years front-load returns.

4. **Environmental restoration is a feature, not a bug.** 10.5 Mtonnes of unlined historic tailings, loss of fish habitat. Mining activities are designed for restoration as much as profit — key driver of social license and permitting success.
