# J.P. Morgan — Fighting Words: The Energy Transition in 2026

## Summary

Michael Cembalest's 16th annual "Eye on the Market" energy paper, dated March 3, 2026. A comprehensive 98-page analysis of energy debates: data center power demand vs grid capacity, solar+storage economics vs natural gas, China's energy dominance, SMR cost viability, geopolitical risk (Iran/Strait of Hormuz), geothermal progress, EV industry losses, and Vaclav Smil's analysis of electrification constraints. The paper is deeply data-driven and takes contrarian positions against several popular energy transition narratives.

## Key Claims

1. **Data centers are driving US power prices up in specific locations.** 75% of PJM's increased capacity payments are directly attributable to data center demand. Data center backlash is underway — at least 25 US projects canceled in 2025 (4x vs 2024). States (TX, OR, CA, GA, OH) are passing legislation to impose higher rates on data centers. BNEF projects data centers will boost US gas demand by 3-4 bcf/day by 2030.
2. **Solar+storage is NOT cost-competitive with gas for baseload power in the US.** Cembalest rebuts EMBER's claims that solar+storage is only 38% more expensive than gas in Las Vegas. EMBER used China-weighted solar costs ($436/kW) for a US city; using US costs ($923/kW + 21% indirect), the real premium is ~3x. Even with 40% ITC subsidies, solar+storage remains more expensive than all-gas systems.
3. **Gas turbine orders are soaring and sold out through 2030.** GE Vernova, Siemens and Mitsubishi each have 20-25% market share. GE Vernova ramping from 16 GW to 26 GW by mid-2028. ERCOT projects 200 GW of new large load requests, 70% from data centers. Global gas turbine orders projected to hit 100+ GW by 2028.
4. **SMRs remain unproven economically.** TVA's first-of-a-kind SMR cost estimate: $18,000/kW → $196/MWh levelized cost. Compare to combined cycle gas at $55-85/MWh. China's first SMR had just 30% availability factor vs 87% for US light-water fleet. NRC independent oversight stripped by Trump executive order — raises safety concerns.
5. **Strait of Hormuz disruption: 20% of global oil flows through it.** US SPR is at historically low levels from Biden-era drawdowns. Oil intensity of GDP has fallen in half since 1990, potentially limiting GDP impact of price spikes. Taiwan and Korea are most exposed.
6. **China dominates everything in energy.** 75% of global solar module production, 85%+ of rare earth processing, 32 nuclear reactors under construction (at 15-35% the cost of Western projects), and now leading on sodium-ion batteries and nuclear fusion research. Chinese green overseas pledges ($225-250B) surpass inflation-adjusted Marshall Plan.
7. **EV industry is mostly profitless.** Only Tesla, BYD, Li Auto, Xiaomi and Seres have positive operating margins. Ford at -72%, GM at -43%, Toyota at -60%. Stellantis took a $26B EV writeoff. Bloomberg EV Index has underperformed S&P 500 Auto Index since April 2023.
8. **US energy independence is real.** 62% of US primary energy from hydraulic fracturing. US is now world's largest LNG exporter. Potential US gas reserves continue to rise (Haynesville/Deep Bossier, Alaska). But US clean energy projects are being canceled under Trump 2.0 at accelerating pace.

## Thesis Impact

- **Impact:** SUPPORTS
- **Tickers affected:** LNG, CCJ, CVX, GEV, CEG, D, BE, ENB, TRP
- **Claims touched:**
  - *SUPPORTS* — Energy overweight thesis. Cembalest's data on soaring gas turbine demand, data center power needs (3-4 bcf/day by 2030), and Strait of Hormuz risk all reinforce the structural case for energy exposure. Natural gas is the clear winner of the AI power buildout.
  - *SUPPORTS* — Geopolitical risk provides a floor under energy prices. The Iran/Strait of Hormuz section directly supports this thesis claim with detailed import sensitivity analysis by country.
  - *MIXED* — Lower oil prices in 2026 (Meldrum's thesis). Cembalest doesn't directly address oil prices but notes US oil demand is stable, SPR is depleted, and Strait of Hormuz disruption adds upside risk. This partially contradicts Meldrum's "lower oil structural" call.
  - *SUPPORTS* — Nuclear/uranium thesis (CCJ on watchlist). Cembalest is skeptical of SMR economics but bullish on conventional nuclear — China building 6-8 plants/year, on track to surpass US by 2030.
  - *NEUTRAL* — AI/Semis thesis. The data center sections confirm AI power demand is real ($700B+ hyperscaler capex) but this is an energy paper, not an AI investment paper.

## What I'm Taking Away

- Natural gas is the undisputed bridge fuel for AI infrastructure. The gas turbine demand data (sold out through 2030) and data center gas demand projections (3-4 bcf/day by 2030) are the strongest data points I've seen for the LNG/gas thesis. This reinforces LNG (Cheniere) on the watchlist.
- The solar+storage vs gas baseload analysis is devastating for the "solar is already cheaper" narrative. Real US costs are 2-3x what EMBER claims. Important context for evaluating utilities thesis (CEG, D on STOCKS.md).
- The Strait of Hormuz section is timely given thesis claim about geopolitical risk providing a floor under energy. The SPR depletion chart is alarming.
- Cembalest's SMR skepticism ($196/MWh first-of-a-kind) means the uranium/nuclear thesis should focus on conventional reactors and existing fleet extensions (CCJ, Cameco) rather than SMR plays (NuScale, Oklo).
- The EV profitability data validates avoiding EV pure-plays. Only BYD and Tesla make money at scale.
- Energy sector +32% YTD (best performing S&P sector per Redpoint) — the thesis energy overweight has been working.
