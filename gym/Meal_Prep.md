# Meal Prep

> 6'1" · 197 lb · Goal: break binge cycle, eat consistently
> Protein target: 150-180g/day · Calorie range: 2200-2600/day (adjust based on activity)
> Prep day: Sunday

---

## Principles

1. **Never restrict during the day** — under-eating is the #1 binge trigger
2. **Eat on schedule, not on hunger** — waiting until "starving" leads to overeating
3. **Pre-plan the evening** — binges happen at night, so that's where the structure goes
4. **No forbidden foods** — restriction creates obsession; plan treats in, don't ban them
5. **High-volume, high-protein meals** — stay physically full so the urge window is shorter
6. **Dead simple cooking** — if it takes more than 10 min active time, you won't do it

---

## Staple Meals

### Protein Oats (breakfast / pre-bed snack)
- 1/2 cup oats, 1 scoop protein powder, 1 cup berries, splash of milk
- ~400 cal · 30g protein · 8g fat · 55g carbs
- *This is your safety meal — always have ingredients on hand*

### Eggs + Potatoes (quick lunch)
- 3 eggs scrambled, 1 medium potato (diced & air fried), hot sauce
- ~450 cal · 25g protein · 20g fat · 35g carbs

### Beef Wraps (grab-and-go — golf, work, car)
- 1 large tortilla, ~5oz ground beef (seasoned), shredded cheese, salsa, handful spinach
- ~600 cal · 40g protein · 28g fat · 40g carbs
- **Prep:** Brown 2 lb ground beef Sunday → portion into bags → assemble wrap in 2 min flat
- *Make 2-3 and wrap in foil. Eat cold or microwave 30 sec. Take to the course.*

### Beef + Rice Bowl (dinner — lazy version)
- 5oz ground beef over 1 cup rice, hot sauce or salsa, shredded cheese
- ~600 cal · 38g protein · 22g fat · 55g carbs
- *Same ground beef from Sunday prep — scoop, microwave, eat*

### Berry Protein Smoothie (snack)
- 1.5 cups frozen berries, 1 scoop protein, 1 cup water/milk
- ~250 cal · 25g protein · 2g fat · 40g carbs

### Greek Yogurt Bowl (evening — binge replacement)
- 1 cup Greek yogurt, handful granola, drizzle honey, berries
- ~350 cal · 25g protein · 8g fat · 45g carbs
- *This is the planned evening snack — eat it BEFORE the urge hits*

## Sunday Prep (20 min)

1. **Brown ground beef** — 2 lb in a pan with taco seasoning or salt/pepper/garlic. Drain, store in one big container. This is your protein source for the whole week.
2. **Cook rice** — big batch (3-4 cups dry), store in a container.
3. **Wrap 2-3 beef wraps in foil** — tortilla + beef + cheese + salsa + spinach. Fridge. Grab on the way out.
4. **Check stock** — berries, oats, yogurt, tortillas. If you're low on any of these, the system breaks.

That's it. No chicken to bake, no potatoes to dice, no portioning yogurt into containers. Brown meat, cook rice, wrap some wraps.

## Anti-Binge Evening Protocol

This is the most important part. Binges follow a pattern:

**Trigger → Urge → Window → Action**

Your known triggers (update as you learn more):
- Watching movies/shows in bed
- Boredom at work leading to under-eating, then overcompensating at night
- Post-nap hunger (real or perceived)
- Having Uber Eats app accessible

**The plan:**
1. Eat the Greek yogurt bowl at **8pm every night** — before the urge, not after
2. After yogurt bowl, brush teeth — creates a psychological "kitchen is closed" signal
3. If still hungry: protein oats (your safety meal) — not a binge, it's a planned backup
4. Delete Uber Eats on Sunday, re-download only if you genuinely need it for a planned meal
5. If you binge anyway: **log it honestly, no guilt, just data** — the log is how you break the cycle

---

## Adjusting Over Time

Review the meal log weekly. Look for:
- Which days had binges? What triggered them?
- Are you eating enough during the day? (If lunch is skipped, evening binges are almost guaranteed)
- Is protein hitting 150g+? (Low protein = less satiety = more cravings)
- What time does the urge typically hit? (Move the yogurt bowl earlier if needed)
- Are the wraps actually getting eaten on the go? If not, swap for something even simpler.
