## What CDE Is

Coeur Mining is a primary silver/gold producer with operations across the US, Mexico, and Canada.
In early 2025 they closed the acquisition of **SilverCrest Metals** (Las Chispas mine, Sonora Mexico)

## Why It's Down -39% Despite Gold at All-Time Highs

1. **Mexico tariff risk** — Las Chispas (Sonora, Mexico) is CDE's crown jewel post-acquisition.
   Any US tariffs on Mexican imports creates operational uncertainty. This is likely the primary
   reason mid-cap Mexican-exposed miners have been punished harder than their metals peers.
2. **Acquisition integration risk** — Markets discount acquirers during integration. The SilverCrest
   deal closed in early 2025; until Las Chispas delivers multiple quarters of clean results,
   the "show me" discount persists.
3. **Miners lag the metal** — A recurring pattern. Gold at ATH doesn't mean miners immediately
   follow; margins, costs, and execution all get repriced first. Cost inflation (energy, labor)
   has eaten into gold miner margins sector-wide.
4. **Small-mid cap hit harder** — Institutional selling in risk-off environments hits mid-caps
   before large-caps (WPM, Agnico, Barrick hold up better by design).
