# XDIV — iShares Core MSCI Canadian Quality Dividend Index ETF (Feb 2026)

## Summary

Official XDIV fact sheet as of February 28, 2026. XDIV is a concentrated 21-holding Canadian dividend ETF that screens for quality (solid balance sheets, steady/increasing dividends). Even more concentrated than XIC — just two sectors (Financials + Energy) account for 73.61% of the fund, and the top 10 holdings are 74.62%.

## Key Claims

1. **Financials 46.10% + Energy 27.51% = 73.61% of XDIV.** This is even more concentrated than XIC (52.75% in Financials + Materials). Owning XDIV means an outsized bet on Canadian banks, insurers, and energy producers.
2. **Only 21 holdings with top 10 at 74.62%.** Suncor (9.12%), TD (9.07%), RY (8.78%), Sun Life (8.60%), Manulife (8.52%) dominate. This is not diversification — it's a concentrated income bet.
3. **IT at 1.55%, Communication at 0.49%.** The cheapest sectors per Morningstar are virtually absent. XDIV amplifies the same XIC concentration problem.
4. **P/E 16.75 and distribution yield 3.69%.** Lower P/E than XIC (21.49) due to value/dividend tilt. The yield is attractive for income but doesn't compensate for sector concentration risk.
5. **1y return 30.24%, 5y annualized 18.54%.** Strong historical performance, but driven by the same financials/energy bull market that Morningstar flags as overvalued.
6. **MER 0.11%.** Very low cost. Appropriate as a dividend income holding if concentration is accepted.

## Thesis Impact

- **Impact:** SUPPORTS
- **Tickers affected:** XDIV.TO (core holding)
- **Claims touched:**
  - *STRONGLY SUPPORTS* — Canadian sector concentration risk thesis. XDIV is even more concentrated than XIC: 73.61% in just two sectors vs. XIC's 52.75%. The satellite strategy is even more critical if XDIV is held alongside XIC.
  - *SUPPORTS* — "Satellite picks should overweight IT and Communication." IT at 1.55% and Communication at 0.49% are virtually absent from XDIV. Any tech/telecom satellite exposure is purely additive.
  - *SUPPORTS* — "Avoid doubling up on banks." TD + RY + Sun Life + Manulife + Power Corp = ~42% of XDIV from financials alone. Holding XDIV + XIC together creates massive financial sector overlap.
  - *NEUTRAL* — Income strategy. The 3.69% yield serves the income objective, which is a valid portfolio function separate from the concentration concern.

## What I'm Taking Away

- XDIV amplifies the XIC concentration problem, not diversifies it. Holding both means ~50%+ in Canadian financials across the combined Canadian allocation.
- The quality/dividend screen produces a decent yield (3.69%) at low P/E (16.75) but at the cost of extreme sector concentration. Worth understanding that the yield comes from overvalued banks and fairly-valued energy names.
- If the satellite strategy adds IT/Communication names (CSU, TRI, BCE, Telus), those positions are doing even more heavy lifting to offset XDIV than they would for XIC alone.
- Restaurant Brands International (QSR) at 5.85% is notable — Morningstar rates it 4-star at 0.89x FV. One of the few XDIV holdings that isn't overvalued.
