# Anatomy of a Ghostly ATH — SPY/QQQ Breadth Analysis

## Summary

A rigorous quantitative analysis of the S&P 500's new all-time high on April 15, 2026 (SPY at 7,022.95). The report decomposes the +12.04% rally from the March 30 low into its constituent-level sources across 503 SPY and 98 QQQ equity constituents. The core finding: the rally is both broad (most names participated, mean EW rally = +6.5%) and catastrophically narrow (index level is recovered solely by mega-cap contribution; almost nothing is back to personal highs). Only 1.75% of SPY by weight is at a 52-week high. This combination is structurally fragile.

## Key Claims

1. **Only 1.75% of SPY (by weight) is at a 52-week high.** Despite the index making a new ATH, the overwhelming majority of constituents haven't recovered their pre-war or late-2025 peaks. The cap-equal gap is +5.49pp for SPY and +6.43pp for QQQ — nearly unprecedented concentration of return within the top of each index.

2. **Mag-7 contributed 50% of SPY's +12.04% rally.** NVDA alone contributed +1.64pp. But heterogeneity within Mag-7 is large: META/AMZN/GOOGL led at +22-25%, while MSFT is -23.8% from 52w high and AAPL barely participated (+8%). The "Mag-7 did it" narrative is really "META/AMZN/GOOGL/NVDA did it."

3. **Energy was the ONLY net detractor to the rally (-0.34pp).** Energy stocks rallied +63% during the war and reversed -10% post-ceasefire. XOM (-0.14pp) and CVX (-0.07pp) were the largest single-stock drags. But energy is STILL +12.2% since Jan 28 — the only sector up double digits on a through-cycle basis.

4. **Tech bifurcation: semiconductor/storage rally vs "SaaSpocalypse."** Within Info Tech (53.3% of SPY's rally), the story is two-fold: INTC +57.7%, SNDK +55.8%, WDC +45%, MU +41.8% (memory/storage boom driven by AI) vs enterprise SaaS getting crushed (NOW -55%, WDAY -55%, GDDY -57%). The SaaSpocalypse aligns with Redpoint's earlier finding.

5. **Credit markets are pricing zero war risk.** HY OAS at 2.84% (below pre-war 2.90%). IG OAS at 0.77% (near multi-decade tights). PineBridge calls it "levels last visited in the mid-1990s" with "little risk premium to absorb shocks." Credit and equity are disagreeing about fragility.

6. **Private credit is the dissenting signal.** Fitch reports 5.8% trailing-12-month private credit default rate (highest since metric began). Redemption gates hit Ares (capped at 5%), Apollo, Blue Owl, BlackRock HPS. PIK interest at 11% of private loans. 26% of direct lending exposed to software borrowers. This is hidden stress beneath the calm public credit markets.

7. **Sector rotation map reveals clear clusters.** Tech/Comm Services/Cons Disc are V-bounce sectors (deep drawdown during war, strong recovery). Energy was a war winner but ceasefire loser. Utilities/Staples are defensive havens (up through war, flat after). Health Care is persistently weak in both windows.

## Thesis Impact

- **Impact:** SUPPORTS
- **Tickers affected:** SPY, NVDA, AVGO, XOM, CVX
- **Claims touched:**
  - *STRONGLY SUPPORTS* — S&P 500 still at 22x fwd PE with extreme concentration risk (top 10 = 41.1%). The "Ghostly ATH" data shows this concentration is even worse than the thesis stated — 1.75% of names by weight at highs, 88% of rally from top 10% of names. The index is fragile.
  - *SUPPORTS* — Energy overweight thesis. Energy is the only sector with a meaningful positive return since Jan 28 (+12.2%) on a through-cycle basis. The post-ceasefire selloff creates a potential re-entry point if the ceasefire collapses (per Citrini's field report, it likely will).
  - *SUPPORTS* — AI/semis selective entry at corrected levels. The memory/storage rally (INTC, SNDK, WDC, MU all +40-58%) confirms the thesis that individual semis have corrected enough for entry. AVGO contributed +1.10pp to the rally — validating the cross-reference #1 ranking.
  - *SUPPORTS* — Credit event risk is elevated. The private credit data (5.8% defaults, redemption gates, PIK at 11%) is the strongest evidence yet that a credit event could be the next catalyst for the "significant market correction (15%+)" trigger.

## What I'm Taking Away

- The ATH is a mirage — only 1.75% of the market is actually at highs. This validates the defensive posture. Do not chase the index level; the breadth says this is not a healthy market.
- Energy's post-ceasefire selloff (XOM -13.1%, OXY -15.7%) creates the re-entry opportunity the thesis was looking for. Combined with Citrini's "ceasefire is fragile" intelligence, this argues for adding to energy on the dip rather than taking profits.
- The private credit stress data should be added to the thesis "What I'm Watching" section. 5.8% default rate + 26% software exposure + redemption gates = a potential credit event catalyst. This is more concerning than public HY/IG spreads suggest.
- AVGO's +1.10pp contribution to the rally and +35.2% rally off low confirms the cross-reference conviction. Still ready for entry per thesis.
