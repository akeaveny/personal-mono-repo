# XIC — iShares Core S&P/TSX Capped Composite Index ETF (Feb 2026)

## Summary

Official XIC fact sheet as of February 28, 2026. This is the reference document for understanding what owning XIC actually means — which sectors, which names, and how concentrated the exposure is. The concentration picture is stark: just two sectors (Financials and Materials) account for over half the fund.

## Key Claims

1. **Financials + Materials = 52.75% of XIC.** This is the single most important fact for the core-satellite thesis. Owning XIC means owning a bet that Canadian banks and gold/copper miners keep outperforming.
2. **IT at 6.91%, Communication at 1.92%.** The two cheapest sectors per Morningstar are the most underweight. Adding satellite exposure here is additive, not redundant with XIC.
3. **Top 10 holdings = 35% of the fund.** Royal Bank alone is 6.5%. Heavily bank-dominated: RY, TD, BMO, CIBC, BNS = 19.12% of the fund just from the Big Five banks.
4. **XIC returned 38.69% over the past year and 31.59% in 2025.** Strong recent performance makes current valuations higher — P/E of 21.49 and P/B of 2.74 are not cheap for a Canadian market index.
5. **218 holdings, MER 0.06%.** Extremely cheap. Appropriate as a core holding despite concentration concerns.
6. **$26.9B AUD in net assets.** Large, liquid, no tracking concerns.

## Thesis Impact

- **Impact:** SUPPORTS
- **Tickers affected:** XIC.TO (core holding)
- **Claims touched:**
  - *STRONGLY SUPPORTS* — Concentration risk thesis. The exact sector weights confirm that owning XIC without satellite positions means ~53% in two sectors Morningstar considers overvalued. This is the foundational argument for why satellite picks matter.
  - *SUPPORTS* — "Satellite picks should overweight IT and Communication." IT at 6.91% and Communication at 1.92% are dramatically underweight relative to their Morningstar valuations. Adding CSU, TRI, BCE, or Telus as satellites explicitly overweights the cheap sectors.
  - *SUPPORTS* — "Avoid doubling up on banks and gold miners." The top 5 banks already represent ~19% of XIC. Any additional Canadian financial or materials position is adding concentration, not diversification.
  - *NEUTRAL* — International diversification argument (XSP/XEF). The XIC concentration makes a stronger case for holding international exposure as the complement.

## What I'm Taking Away

- XIC is a bet on Canadian financials and gold miners continuing to outperform. Both sectors are Morningstar 1-2 star. The satellite strategy is precisely about offsetting this tilt.
- The fund's strong 1-year return (38.69%) may create complacency — but Morningstar's midcycle gold price estimate ($2,050 vs. $5,000 spot) implies significant mean-reversion risk in the Materials portion.
- Shopify is now 4.09% of XIC — so buying SHOP separately would be doubling up on an already meaningful position. Check overlap before adding any individual TSX names.
- The 0.06% MER is genuinely exceptional. No reason to replace XIC as the Canadian core — just complement it.
