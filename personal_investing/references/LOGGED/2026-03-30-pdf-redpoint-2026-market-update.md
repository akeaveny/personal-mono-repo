# Redpoint Ventures — 2026 Market Update

## Summary

68-slide deck from Redpoint Ventures (March 2026) covering the state of AI investment, the SaaS selloff, and where value is accruing. The central tension: AI infrastructure spending is massive and real (not a bubble), but it's destroying public SaaS multiples as investors price in terminal value compression from AI disruption. Horizontal SaaS is down 35% LTM while vertical SaaS and infrastructure are flat. The report argues we're in the historical sweet spot (year 4-5 of a platform shift) for new company formation.

## Key Claims

1. **AI capex is not dotcom 2.0.** Hyperscaler capex estimated at $700B+ in 2026 (36% CAGR 2024-2027). Key differences from 2000: revenue follows capex this time (OpenAI $25B ARR, Anthropic $19B ARR), funded by cash flow not debt, >90% of data center capacity is pre-committed, NVIDIA EPS ~$4.06 vs. Cisco's peak $0.37.
2. **Public SaaS is at lowest multiples ever.** Median NTM revenue multiple at 4.1x as of 3/20/2026. Software sector down 20% YTD — worst performing S&P 500 sector. Energy is the best at +32% YTD.
3. **Horizontal SaaS down 35% LTM, vertical SaaS +3%, infrastructure +2%.** The selloff is highly differentiated. Horizontal apps are "accidentally optimized for replaceability" — AI solves coordination problems natively. Vertical SaaS owns irreplaceable data + compliance moats. Infrastructure (Snowflake, Datadog, Cloudflare) benefits from more AI deployment.
4. **Terminal value compression is the real issue, not near-term earnings.** 85-95% of SaaS enterprise value is terminal value. Quarterly beats don't move the needle when investors are questioning 10-year moats. The newspaper analogy is ominous — newspaper earnings held up for years before collapsing.
5. **Agent maturity curve is still early.** Currently in "Task Agents" phase (minutes of independent runtime). TAM expands from $0.5T (US software) → $1.2T (+services automation) → $2.8T (+operational payroll) → $6.1T+ (+knowledge payroll). The market today is pricing in disruption risk before disruption has fully arrived.
6. **54% of CIOs pursuing vendor consolidation; 45% of AI budgets replacing existing software budgets.** But 54% of CIOs still prefer incumbents adding AI over AI-native vendors. Incumbents get preferential treatment but are "largely missing the boat" on execution (Agentforce, Copilot both disappointing per CIO surveys).
7. **Private market AI valuations look crazy but growth-adjusted are actually cheap.** Series B/C median ARR multiples at 61.1x vs. public high-growth at 9.7x (528% premium). But growth-adjusted: private at 0.05x vs. public at 0.37x (86% discount). Median B/C ARR growth is 640%.
8. **OpenAI projecting $113B revenue by 2028E; Anthropic projecting $102B by 2028E.** Current run-rates: $25B and $19B respectively. If these projections hold, foundation model labs become among the largest software companies ever within 3 years.

## What I'm Taking Away

- The SaaS selloff is creating real value in specific categories. Vertical SaaS and infrastructure companies with AI tailwinds (Datadog, Cloudflare, Snowflake) are being unfairly punished alongside horizontal SaaS that genuinely faces disruption risk.
- The thesis trigger for upgrading AI/Semis from "Neutral" to "Overweight" is partially firing: OpenAI and Anthropic are generating $25B and $19B ARR respectively. This is real revenue, not just capex announcements. Consider whether the semis watchlist (NVDA, AVGO, MRVL) deserves re-evaluation.
- The newspaper analogy is worth internalizing: earnings can hold up for years before structural disruption hits. This applies to Canadian banks (Morningstar's "don't extrapolate trading income" warning) as much as to SaaS.
- SNOW is worth a deeper look — it's on the "Look Into" list in STOCKS.md and sits in the infrastructure layer that Redpoint says benefits from AI adoption.
- Software sector at -20% YTD while Energy at +32% YTD is a striking divergence. The thesis's energy overweight has worked in 2026 so far, even if the structural case is weakening per Meldrum.
