# TD Cowen — Initiating Coverage of US Autos & Mobility (Ahead of the Curve Series)

## Summary

TD Cowen initiates coverage on 17 US auto and auto parts companies in their "Ahead of the Curve" series (March 6, 2025). The report is comprehensive (~200 pages) with proprietary data including a US vehicle density survey, county-level EV adoption models, and GM EV market share tracking. Core thesis: auto stocks are at historically depressed valuations, making the sector ripe for stock picking. AV/AI could expand the available automaker profit pool by ~3x. GM is the top pick with a Street-high $105 price target.

## Key Claims

1. **GM is the top pick with a Street-high $105 price target (133% upside from $45).** GM's earnings are dominated by the North America pickup truck franchise (~$90 SoP, ~$100B segment), which has structural demand growth, concentrated competition, and mid-teen operating margins. GM is leaning into EVs at a time when others are pulling back — Cadillac's luxury EV share could reach ~20%. 10% FCF yield at current prices.

2. **AV/AI could ~3x the automaker addressable profit pool.** Traditional automakers capture only ~40% of a vehicle's lifetime revenue. Consumer AVs (hands/eyes-off, L4) could unlock the remaining ~60% through new business models: RoboTaxi, AV trucking, delivery, logistics, and recurring software/services revenue. Tesla is the leader, with Aptiv/Mobileye as top supplier plays.

3. **US auto demand is more resilient than consensus.** TD Cowen's proprietary vehicle density survey shows more pent-up demand than commonly appreciated. Midwest and South are the strongest regions for go-forward demand, which benefits GM and the Detroit 3. US auto sales expected to recover to 16.5M this year and 17.1M next year.

4. **Supplier stocks are close to bottoming.** The bear case on suppliers (China mix pressure, production declines, margin headwinds) has largely played out. H2 2025/2026 should see easing mix pressures, bottoming production, and margin recovery. Aptiv is the top supplier pick ($90 PT, 43% upside), followed by Autoliv and Visteon.

5. **Mexico/Canada tariffs assumed short-lived.** Base case is tariffs last weeks-to-months, not permanently. Permanent tariffs would severely impact the entire industry. Tesla is less exposed to tariffs than peers.

6. **Don't sleep on the US EV market.** US vehicle density sets up a realistic scenario where EV demand could quickly inflect if consumers shift to 1 ICE / 1 EV per household. Top US EV counties saw rapid penetration to >20% in just a few years. TD Cowen expects US EV penetration to rise from 8% to 12% in coming years.
