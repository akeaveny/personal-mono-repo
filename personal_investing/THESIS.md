# 2026 Investing Thesis

Personal investing thesis guiding the Core-Satellite portfolio strategy.

---

## Market Outlook: Defensive

The overall posture for 2026 is **defensive**. After an extended bull run driven by AI hype and multiple expansion, the risk/reward for broad equities has deteriorated. The focus is on capital preservation, steady income, and selective positioning for when opportunities emerge.

**Core conviction:** Protect capital first. Stay diversified. Be patient and wait for better entry points rather than chasing momentum.

---

## Macro Environment

### Geopolitical Risk — Elevated
- US-China tensions continue to drive uncertainty across trade, tech supply chains, and capital flows
- Tariff policy is unpredictable and creates sector-level volatility
- Global supply chain reshoring is inflationary in the short term but necessary long-term
- Energy security is increasingly tied to geopolitical stability

### Recession Risk — Real and Underpriced
- Consumer spending showing signs of fatigue after years of post-COVID normalization
- Labor market softening — hiring slowing, layoffs ticking up in certain sectors
- Credit conditions tightening, especially for small/mid-cap businesses
- Yield curve dynamics still warrant caution
- The market may be pricing in a soft landing that doesn't materialize

---

## Sector Views

### Energy / Commodities — Overweight (High Conviction)
- Supply constraints persist: underinvestment in oil/gas exploration, aging infrastructure, OPEC+ discipline
- Geopolitical risk provides a floor under energy prices — any escalation is a catalyst
- Commodities broadly benefit from deglobalization and reshoring trends
- Energy stocks offer real cash flows and dividends in a defensive environment
- Uranium deserves attention as nuclear gains policy support globally

### AI / Semiconductors — Neutral (Watching)
- The AI infrastructure buildout is real, but valuations have run far ahead of monetization
- Waiting to see how the spending cycle plays out — capex has surged but revenue follow-through is unclear
- Risk of an "AI winter" narrative if earnings don't justify multiples
- Semiconductors are cyclical — a broader slowdown would hit them regardless of AI tailwinds
- Not selling existing positions, but not adding until the picture clarifies
- **Trigger to upgrade:** meaningful revenue acceleration from AI products, or a significant pullback in valuations

### Big Tech / Cloud — Neutral
- Strong businesses, but concentration risk in the S&P is a concern
- Cloud growth is decelerating from peak rates
- Regulatory risk increasing (antitrust, AI regulation)
- Already well-represented in core ETF holdings (VTI) — no need to double up in satellite

## Portfolio Strategy

### Core (80%) — Stay the Course
The core ETF allocation is designed to weather any environment:

| ETF | Role | Why It Fits the Thesis |
|-----|------|----------------------|
| VTI | US Total Market | Broad exposure, tilts defensive in downturns via diversification |
| VXUS | International | Diversification away from US concentration risk |
| BND | US Bonds | Capital preservation, income, recession hedge |
| VNQ | REITs | Real assets, inflation hedge, income |

**No changes to core targets.** The defensive posture is expressed through the satellite allocation, not by timing the core.

### Satellite (20%) — Selective, Patient
- **Overweight energy/commodities** — this is where active conviction gets expressed
- **Hold but don't add to AI/semis** — wait for either a pullback or earnings confirmation
- **Keep cash buffer** — it's okay to be under-allocated in satellite if nothing meets the bar
- **Min position size discipline** — don't spread too thin; 3-5 satellite positions max

## Principles

1. **Don't force trades.** If nothing screams opportunity, holding cash in the satellite bucket is a valid position.
2. **Respect the drift threshold.** Let the bot flag when rebalancing is needed rather than reacting to daily noise.
3. **Thesis reviews are quarterly.** Revisit this document every quarter and update the sector views.
4. **Core never changes on a whim.** The 80% ETF base is the foundation — only adjust targets after a full thesis review.
5. **Size positions to sleep at night.** No single satellite position should cause stress if it drops 30%.
