# Banyan Gold Corp (BYN-TSX) — Taking A Stab At The AurMac PEA

## Summary

Cormark Securities initiating coverage report on Banyan Gold (BYN-TSX), a gold exploration company in the Yukon's Tintina Gold Belt. With a maiden PEA for the AurMac project expected in H2 2026, Cormark built a mine model projecting 5 MMoz mineable resource at 0.80 g/t, 40,000 tpd throughput, 301K oz/yr average production over 15 years. NAV jumps from C$1.55 to C$6.00 on the new DCF methodology. Buy rating with C$3.00 target (146% upside from C$1.22).

## Key Claims

1. **AurMac is a large-scale, low-grade open pit gold project.** 5.0 MMoz mineable at 0.80 g/t, 3.0:1 strip ratio. Higher grades (1.0 g/t) in years 1-4, declining thereafter. 40,000 tpd mill throughput, 90% recovery. Total resource now 8.2 MMoz.

2. **Economics are strong at $4,000/oz gold.** NPV5% of C$4.37B, IRR of 48.8%. Initial capex C$1.5B, sustaining capex C$600M. Cash costs $1,400/oz, AISC $1,545/oz. Early years (1-4) are significantly better: cash costs $1,262/oz, AISC $1,380/oz.

3. **Massive valuation discount persists.** Trading at 0.20x NAV (C$1.22 vs C$6.00 NAV). At spot gold ($4,593/oz), NAV rises to C$7.55 (0.16x P/NAV). Unfunded/undiluted takeout NAV is C$9.90.

4. **Strong balance sheet for an explorer.** C$38.8M cash, zero debt. Will need ~30% equity financing for capex (modeled at 368.9M new shares at C$1.22).
