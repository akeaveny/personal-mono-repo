# Wicked 3rd qtr GDP and 2026 Investment Themes

## Summary

Mark Meldrum breaks down Q3 2025 GDP (4.3% headline — inflated by tariff-reversal trade flows, not a signal of underlying strength) and then outlines 8 thematic calls for 2026 with specific portfolio positioning. The overall tilt: overweight materials and financials, underweight energy and consumer staples, neutral via SPY, long FCX and IWM.

## Key Claims

1. **Q3 GDP 4.3% is noise, not signal.** The beat came from trade flows reversing tariff-era stockpiling (imports down $44B, exports up $56B). This does not repeat in Q4.
2. **Fed funds rate is materially mispriced.** ZQ futures have ~2 cuts priced over 8 meetings. Meldrum expects far more; Powell is leaving and the Bessant/Worsh team will push cuts harder.
3. **USD weakness continues → metals strength.** Metals priced in USD, so weaker dollar = higher prices in other currencies without fundamental change. FCX is his top pick (20%+ expected gain in 2026).
4. **AI shifts from "does it work?" to "show me the revenue."** 2025 proved effectiveness. 2026 is about monetization: ads are arriving (Gemini, OpenAI). Revenue tail starts mattering. Supports QQQ.
5. **Lower oil prices in 2026.** Driven by: more EVs globally, walking oil rigs (higher output per capex dollar), and a likely Venezuela oil deal. Underweight energy and consumer staples.
6. **IPO boom incoming.** 2026 resembles 1999 in IPO volume — QQQ gets a lift from new tech listings.
7. **Crypto failures accelerate.** Bitcoin has no use case with stable coins in place. Expects .com-style collapse across crypto. Negative on Bitcoin miners (HUT, Riot, IREN).
8. **Weight loss drug adoption continues** to grow; still a meaningful secular theme.
